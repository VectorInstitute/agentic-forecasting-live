As of March 31, 2026, the S&P/TSX Composite sits at an interesting juncture, having navigated a volatile Q1 characterized by a "wait-and-see" Bank of Canada (BoC) and strong performance in the resource-heavy sectors. Analysts are debating the following scenarios for the next 60 business days.

## Scenario: Commodity-Led Defensive Rotation (~0.55)
**Key drivers:** This scenario assumes that geopolitical friction in the Middle East persists, keeping oil prices elevated and safe-haven demand for gold strong. With the BoC holding steady at 2.25% and domestic economic data signaling a "soft landing" (unemployment holding near 6.8%), investors continue to rotate into the TSX's heavy-hitting Energy and Materials sectors. The modest, stable yield environment in Canada, compared to potential volatility from US trade-policy headlines, keeps domestic capital focused on high-dividend, resource-based "defensive" equity.
**~60-day outlook:** A steady, grinding move higher in the TSX index, likely in the +3% to +5% range.

## Scenario: US-Trade Policy Stalls/Tariff Risk (~0.30)
**Key drivers:** This scenario posits that the "wait-and-see" comfort is broken by aggressive US trade rhetoric or the implementation of specific trade barriers targeting Canadian exports. Even if commodity prices remain stable, the valuation discount on the Canadian market widens as the market prices in a "tariff premium." The Canadian dollar (USD/CAD) faces downward pressure, and foreign investors pull back from Canadian cyclical stocks, fearing a slowdown in industrial output and trade-exposed sectors like Transport and Industrials.
**~60-day outlook:** A retracement of recent gains, with a drawdown of 4% to 7% as risk-off sentiment takes hold.

## Scenario: BoC Policy Disappointment (~0.15)
**Key drivers:** This scenario assumes that the Bank of Canada, faced with signs of domestic economic cooling that turn out to be more severe than expected, shifts its rhetoric from "wait-and-see" to "active easing." While usually bullish, a sudden shift in policy signal at this stage could spook the market, raising fears that the central bank sees a deeper recessionary threat than previously acknowledged. The focus shifts from sector rotation to macro-fragility, leading to a liquidity-driven correction across both growth and value sectors.
**~60-day outlook:** High volatility, potentially settling in a flat to slightly negative (-2% to +1%) range as the market digests the pivot.

## Base case
The **Commodity-Led Defensive Rotation** is the base case. The TSX is structurally anchored by Energy and Materials; provided there is no acute shock from US trade policy, the market’s current propensity is to lean into the yield and commodity-price support that these sectors provide. The BoC's explicit "wait-and-see" approach reinforces this stability, as investors have a predictable policy path for the next few months, encouraging a focus on sector-specific fundamentals over macro-policy surprises.
