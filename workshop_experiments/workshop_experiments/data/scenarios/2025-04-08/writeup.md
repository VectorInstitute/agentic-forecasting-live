## Scenario: The "Soft-Landing" Pivot (~0.60)
**Key drivers:** The Bank of Canada, having paused its aggressive cutting cycle in early 2025, observes inflation stabilizing near the 2% target despite stubborn wage growth (3.6% in Q1). Investors interpret this as a successful "soft landing," leading to a compression in GoC bond yields as the market prices in a more stable, neutral policy rate environment. Oil prices remain supported by OPEC+ discipline, and the TSX, led by the Financials and Energy sectors, sees a stabilization in valuations as recession fears recede.
**~60-day outlook:** A recovery from recent lows, with potential for a return to flat or a modest positive return of +2% to +4% as market sentiment improves.

## Scenario: The "Stagflationary" Stall (~0.40)
**Key drivers:** Persistently higher-than-expected wage growth (3.6% in Q1) and cooling employment (6.6% unemployment) trigger concerns that the Canadian economy is entering a period of stagflation. The BoC is forced to signal a "higher for longer" policy stance, preventing bond yields from falling. Simultaneously, base metals face renewed downward pressure due to cooling global industrial demand, and the Canadian dollar weakens further against the USD, adding imported inflationary pressure and hurting sentiment for domestically focused stocks.
**~60-day outlook:** Continued volatility and a potential further drawdown of 3% to 5%, as the market struggles with earnings headwinds and persistent macro uncertainty.

## Base case
**The "Soft-Landing" Pivot**
Despite the recent sharp drawdown (the -0.05 log return on April 4th and -0.02 on April 8th), the underlying fundamental backdrop—oil prices remaining supported and the BoC having already proactively managed the rate cycle—suggests that this is a correction within a broader transition to a more stable growth environment. Markets historically tend to overreact to the initial "cool down" phase of a pivot; unless we see a major spike in structural unemployment or a collapse in oil, a stabilization is the most probable path for the TSX index.
