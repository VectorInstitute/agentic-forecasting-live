## Scenario: The "Trade Conflict Standoff" (~0.55)
**Key drivers:** The primary driver is a prolonged stalemate in U.S.-Canada trade relations, characterized by the persistence of the 25% U.S. tariffs on non-CUSMA goods and Canadian retaliatory measures. The Bank of Canada (BoC) adopts a "wait-and-see" pause after their March 12, 2025, rate cut, as Governing Council members focus on mitigating inflation shocks from trade friction while balancing the cooling domestic labour market. Persistent economic uncertainty leads to suppressed business investment and capital expenditure (capex) across Canada.

**~60-day outlook:** Flat to slightly lower, in a range of -3% to +1%. The index remains supported by gold's safe-haven status, but gains are capped by tariff-driven headwinds hitting growth-sensitive sectors like industrials and consumer discretionary.

## Scenario: "Commodity-Led Divergence" (~0.30)
**Key drivers:** A material-sector breakout ensues as geopolitical instability (e.g., escalating conflict or supply chain shocks) drives gold prices well above US$3,100/oz. This performance masks broader domestic weakness caused by the tariff-induced slowdown. The Canadian dollar remains under heavy pressure due to the persistent interest rate spread between the BoC and the Federal Reserve, which, while beneficial for exporters, exacerbates imported inflation and keeps domestic consumption weak.

**~60-day outlook:** Bullish for the index, potential +3% to +6%, driven entirely by the outsized weighting of the Materials sector and energy producers holding up against demand concerns.

## Scenario: "Tariff-Driven Earnings Downgrade" (~0.15)
**Key drivers:** The reality of the tariff impact finally breaches corporate earnings reports. Increased cost-of-goods-sold (COGS) pressures combined with a weakening consumer lead to significant downward revisions in S&P/TSX earnings guidance for the second half of 2025. This forces the BoC to abandon its pause and pivot back to an aggressive easing bias, but the market reacts negatively as the policy response is viewed as "too little, too late" to prevent a technical recession.

**~60-day outlook:** Bearish, a drawdown of 5% to 8% as valuations contract to reflect heightened risk and weaker future profitability.

## Base case
The **"Trade Conflict Standoff"** is the base case. The TSX is currently navigating a period of high uncertainty where domestic policy is locked in a defensive posture against U.S. trade policy. The lack of a clear resolution to the tariff dispute suggests that markets will remain range-bound, oscillating between fear of economic contraction and the comfort provided by gold-linked materials and defensive sectors. The market is not yet pricing in a deep recession, nor is there a clear catalyst for a breakout beyond continued strength in precious metals.
